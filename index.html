Privacy policy

Last modified: December 2, 2017

Information we collect

There are three categories of information we collect:
Information you provide

Information we can get when you use our services

Information we collect from third parties

Information you provide

When you interact with our services, we collect the information that you choose to share with us.
Registration. When you register through the app, you provide us with a verified email address and an optional phone number. During the process of registering, we will you an email to verify your email address. If you elect to register your phone number, we will send you a verification code via SMS to verify it.

Nickname. By default, your verified email address will be used as your nickname across the app. You can change it anytime in the Settings section of the app.

Profile picture. If you want you can also add a profile picture to make it easier and more fun to interact with your friends.

Address book. We also allow you to share your address book to easily find friends that are already active on Lifetime. Sharing your address book also makes it easy to invite friends to Lifetime.

Location. Lifetime is a location­based service and in order for the app to fully function we need permission to collect your precise location when you use the app. We only use your location to provide you with localized recommendations and when you attempt to check-in a nearby place. In any case, if you do not chose to check-in, your location will never be transmitted to any other user in the app. We determine your location using methods that include GPS, wireless networks, cell towers, WiFi access points.

Places. You can create public and private places in the app at anytime. The creation process is localization-based which means you can only create a place at your current location. A private place is accessible only by your friends, whereas a public place (any category other than ‘private’) is accessible by anyone. Lifetime may decide to delete or alter your created place at anytime, thus enabling a smooth evolution of the app database and deleting any inappropriate content.

Communications. You also provide us with any other information you send through our services, such as messages.

Information we can get when you use our services

When you use our services, we collect information about how you use them, here are some examples: Usage information. We collect information about your activity through our service, for example: how you communicate with your friends on Lifetime, such as their usernames, time and date of your communications, and also of the places you frequent, such as your favorite places, the one you’ve been to recently, and the one you wish to go to as well as the private places you have created and visited. We may collect information that other users provide about you when they use our services. For example, if another user allows us to collect information from the address book on their phone ­ and you’re one of that user’s contacts ­ we may combine the information we collect from that user’s address book with other information we have collected about you. e

How we use information

Our main goal is to offer the best possible service so you and your friends are able to connect with each other wherever and whenever based on your current location and the places you go to. Specifically, we may use your information to: - develop, operate, improve, deliver, maintain, and protect our services. - send you communications, including by email. For example, we may use email to respond to support inquiries or to share information about services. - create statistical studies and monitor and analyze trends (all studies and analysis are run on an anonymized basis). - enhance the safety and security of our products and services. - send you push notification. If you don't want to receive push notifications, just go to your settings to turn them off. - verify your identity and prevent fraud or other unauthorized or illegal activity. - enforce our Terms of Service and other usage policies.

How we share information

We may share your information in the following ways: With other Lifetime users. We may share the following information with other Lifetime users: - information about you such as your nickname, and status as well as your profile picture - if they are your friends: your location, your friend and follower lists, the places you have visited in Lifetime (check-in actions) as well as the time you’ve spent there, plus your favorite and private places and your wish list. - information about how you have interacted with the services, such as the day you joined or became friends, the number of places you have visited in Lifetime. - any other information you have consented for us to share. For example, when you let us access your device address book, we may share information about you with other users who have your name, email address and phone number in their device address book. With third parties. We may share your information with the following third parties: With service providers and partners. We may share information about you with service providers who perform services on our behalf and business partners that provide services and functionality. With third parties for legal reasons. We may share information about you if we reasonably believe that disclosing the information is needed to: - comply with any valid legal process, governmental request, or applicable law, rule, or regulation. - investigate, remedy, or enforce potential Terms of Service violations. - protect the rights, property, and safety of us, our users, or others. - detect and resolve any fraud or security concerns.

How long we keep your information

We store your information as long as necessary and as long as you’re a user of our service. We have different retention protocols in place depending on the information. As a general rule, we store the information we collect as long as you are actively using the Lifetime app. If you decide to delete your account, all your information will be completely deleted and wiped out of our database. Nevertheless we also aggregate or anonymize information for certain purposes such as data analytics. Once we've anonymized the data we retain the data indefinitely.
